<objective>
Replace the auto-deduced travel range (currently derived from child heights with a brittle `paintSize.shortestSide / 4` fallback) with an explicit `travelRange` field on `ElevatorConfig` ∈ [0.0, 1.0], expressed as a fraction of bbox height. Default 1.0 — restores the pre-260511-dxa "platform travels the full headroom" visual.

Purpose: A child's WIDTH was indirectly shrinking platform travel because the fallback for an unset child height was `shortestSide / 4`. For a tall narrow elevator that became `bboxWidth / 4` — small, and confusingly correlated with width changes. The fix is to stop inferring travel from children at all: the operator picks it explicitly via a slider, with a sensible default (1.0 = full range).

Output: New `travelRange` field on `ElevatorConfig`, regenerated codegen, a slider in the config editor, removed auto-deduce in `_buildStack`, updated tests, and regenerated widget-level goldens (`elevator_with_children_progress_50.png` and `..._100.png`). Painter-level goldens (`position_*.png`, `stale.png`, `position_50_out_of_range.png`) and `elevator_with_children_progress_0.png` are byte-identical and stay untouched.
</objective>

  double progress,
  double bboxHeight,
